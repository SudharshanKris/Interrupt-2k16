CODE NINJA


Brace yourself for an exciting event as we unravel the mysteries of two of the most popular programming languages, C and C++! If you're a coder, there is no better place for you than Code Ninja!
It's time to showcase your coding skills while we take you through the rounds. Solve puzzles and crack code to find your way to glory!

Round 1
The first round consists of objective type questions related to the concepts of C and C++. Choose the correct option or enter the answers to the questions in the space provided. Get a top score to slide into Round 2!

Round 2
Participants will recieve 15 questions, 5 easy, 5 medium and 5 hard and a challenge question. This round is flexible, participants get to choose the questions that they have to solve and the number of questions that they have to solve.
Participants are scored as follows:

  • Easy-20
  • Medium-30
  • Hard-50
  • Challenge-100
The first participant/team to reach 100 points wins!

Rules
1)You may code in C or Java only.
2)You may participate as team of two or alone.
3)The decision of the coordinators is final.

DON OF LOGIC

How would you survive against a wild bear if you just have just a blanket, a broomstick and an ice-cream sandwich? Can you decrypt coded messages? Can you come up with algorithms on the fly to save the day?

If your answer to any of the above questions is yes, then only one thing remains to be decided. Are you worthy of being the Don of Logic?

Round 1: Written Test
You have 30 minutes to answer 20 lateral thinking questions. 16 possible Dons will move to the next round.

Round 2: Quiz
Each Don-hopeful is given 5 cards. The card will have a daily household object like a blanket or a broomstick. There is one special (Wild) card, which has all the objects (Tools). The quiz master poses a survival question, and each player has to take out an answer card and place it face down. Once all the players have done so, each player has to show what tool they used and how they used that tool. Only if the explanation is correct will the player be awarded a point. Each player will now be given a new card (can be any tool) from the pack. The first six players to reach 13 points advance to the final round.
The first six players to reach 13 points advance to the final round.
Round 3: Coding with a twist
Finalists will be given a question and 45 minutes to write, compile and execute a simple program the answers the question. The program can be written in C or C++ as per the participant’s choice. The winner of this round is the of Don Of Logic!

Do you ponder upon intricacies while learning about technology? Do you search for solutions to the problems of this gadget dependent world? Do you dream of joining that elite league of the innovators? If so, then collate your ideas into a presentation, collaborate with a like minded person and come showcase it at Interrupt!

Topics
- Embedded systems
- Computer Networks
- Artificial intelligence
- Big data
- Internet of things
- Cloud computing
- Image processing
- Other computer science related topics

Rules
1.Maximum number of participants in a team is 3.
2. It is advisable that the paper presentation focuses on one particular topic.
3. Abstract should not exceed 80 words. Paper should not exceed 600 words but should have minimum of 450 words.
4. Paper should be in IEEE format.
5. Paper should be submitted as a word document (.doc,.docx,.odt) or pdf.
6. Plagiarism is strictly prohibited.
7. Bring your PowerPoint presentation in a pen drive.
8. Mail your abstract and full paper to svcepapier2k16@gmail.com on or before 25/09/2016.

10. Selected candidates should bring two hardcopies of paper.
11. The teams will get minimum of 7 minutes and maximum of 10 minutes for presentation followed by question and answer.

13. The submission should contain:
- Title-theme of the paper.

-phone number of both the authors.
- E-mail ID.
14. Shortlisted papers will be notified through mail.
